@elonmusk: Style and Influence

An analysis of Elon Musk's tweets and their influence on markets and pop culture.

Introduction

Elon Musk is the richest man in the world and showcases his eccentric personality as an avid Twitter user. He frequently tweets about his companies Tesla and SpaceX, his advocacy for cryptocurrency, and the platform Twitter itself. We will take a look at Musk's Twitter style and analyze potential correlations between his tweets and relevant stock prices and Google search trends.

Tweet Freqency and Interactions

These visualizations show Elon Musk's Tweet frequency by month (1-12) and Tweet frequency by day (1-31). From the Tweet Frequency vs. Month plot, we can see that he Tweets the most during July and May, respectively. Musk's tweet frequency is generally higher during the first half of the year compared to the latter half of the year, tweeting the least amount in September. The Tweet Frequency vs. Day plot shows that Musk's most popular Tweet date is the 25th of the month. The distribution is roughly uniform, indicating roughly consistent tweeting throughout each month.

The next visualizations show Elon Musk's Twitter likes, retweets, and replies from 2010 to March 2022. The upper plots break up the distribution by year while the lower plots break up the distribution by day. The likes, retweets, and replies show similar trends in both the upper and lower sets. The plots show that Elon Musk had an explosion of growth in Twitter popularity, especially starting from 2016. Musk is currently on pace to have more Twitter interactions in the year 2022.

The two graphs above use different forms of sentiment analysis to quantify the sentiment of Musk's tweets. The upper Tweet Subjectivity vs. Polarity graph uses the TextBlob sentiment analyzer to find two values per tweet: (1) The subjectivity, which measures how opinionated or objective the tweet is, and (2) the polarity, which measures how emotionally positive or negative the tweet is. You can hover over a point on the plot to view the corresponding tweet. The Tweet Frequency vs. VADER Polarity graph uses VADER (Valence Aware Dictionary and sEntiment Reasoner) analysis to show the frequency of the positive or negative sentiments with scores between -1 and 1. VADER is optimized for social media data.

Both graphs show that Musk mainly tweets with positive sentiments. The upper plot features a roughly inverted bell curve, with most of his tweets falling near zero polarity and zero subjectivity. The data on the bottom plot is skewed left; The highest concentration of his tweets have a VADER score between +0.4 and +0.45. An independent Python calculation found the ratio between positive and negative tweets to be 3.59 using TextBlob and 3.30 using VADER. These results further emphasize that Musk has a higher concentration of positive tweets than negative tweets.

Tweets and the Stock Market

The four graphs above show the stock price over time, the stock's moving 50 day average, and the frequency of Musk's tweets related to each stock for four relevant stocks: Bitcoin, Dogecoin, Tesla, and Twitter. All values are normalized for more effective comparison. The time ranges are set based on relevancy of the stock and Musk's tweets.

The Bitcoin graph shows correlation between Musk's Twitter activity and Bitcoin's stock trends. From late 2020 to late 2021, Musk's Bitcoin-related tweet frequency increased right around the same times Bitcoin experienced rapid gains and losses. One example comes on May 12, 2021 when Musk announced on Twitter that Tesla would no longer accept Bitcoin as payment. Shortly after, Bitcoin dropped nearly 15%.

The Dogecoin graph also shows correlation between early 2021 and 2022. Up to Dogecoin's peak in May 2021, Musk had been tweeting about it with high frequency between January and May. Dogecoin surged by 50% after Musk tweeted "Dogecoin is the people's crypto" along with this doge-themed image:

The Tesla graph shows the similarly sporadic tweet frequencies and Tesla prices. While the vizualization itself shows no obvious correlation, a look at specific instances lends credence to a correlation. For example, on May 1, 2020, Musk tweeted "Tesla stock price is too high imo," leading to Tesla stock dropping 10% soon after. On April Fools Day 2018, Musk tweeted that "Tesla has gone completely and totally bankrupt," which led to Tesla stock dropping by more than 8%.

The Twitter graph shows little correlation between his tweets and Twitter stock. Given the current events with Musk's acquisition of Twitter, perhaps data for more recent dates would show a correlation.

Tweets and Google Trends

The graphs above show the Google Search trends for "Bitcoin," "Dogecoin," "Elon Musk," "Tesla," and "SpaceX" plotted on top of Musk's tweet frequency related to the respective topic. All data is normalized for more effective comparison.

The Bitcoin graph shows strong correlation between Musk's tweets and Bitcoin's search popuarity. Almost all of Musk's highest frequency days align with or near local maximums in Google searches.

The Dogecoin graph shows a correlation, as well. The highest peaks in Google searches coincide with high frequencies of Musk's Dogecoin tweets. Local maximums in searches after July 2021 also mostly occur near to a number of Dogecoin tweets.

Unlike the other four graphs, the Elon Musk graph plots the frequency of all his tweets. Interestingly, the highest peak in tweet frequency aligns with the highest peak in Google Search trends. However, it is difficult to discern a clear correlation from this visualization as it is a sporadic distribution.

The Tesla graph shows a slight correlation between Musk's Tesla-related tweet frequency and Tesla search trends. As Musk's tweet frequency got more dense over time, search trends generally increased. During March 2018, April 2019, Feb 2020, Jul 2020, Nov 2021, and April 2022, leaps in search trends coincided with leaps in tweet frequency.

The SpaceX graph shows no correlation between search trends and Musk's SpaceX-related tweet frequency. Despite a massive number of tweets from April 2018 to April 2020, SpaceX search trends saw no massive change.
